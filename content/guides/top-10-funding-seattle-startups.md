## #1: Seattle Angel Conference ($150K-$200K)

The most significant single investment opportunity for Seattle startups.

### What It Is

An NCAA-style pitch competition where 30-40 angel investors pool their capital to invest in one winner.

### Details

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Prize | $150,000-$200,000 investment |
| Format | 3-month competition |
| Investors | 30-40 per cohort |
| Running since | 2012 (14 years) |
| Total invested | $4.6M in 39 companies |

### Recent Success

Latest investment: $1.5M Seed round in dopl (February 2025)

### How to Apply

1. Apply when applications open (typically fall)
2. Compete through multiple rounds
3. Final 4 companies pitch to full investor group
4. Winner receives pooled investment

### Best For

- Startups ready for angel investment
- Companies with traction
- Founders who can pitch well

## #2: Seattle Capital Access Program (Up to $30K)

City of Seattle program that pays down your loan principal.

### What It Is

The city will pay up to 20% of your loan principal (maximum $30,000) when you borrow from a participating lender.

### Details

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Benefit | 20% loan principal reduction |
| Maximum | $30,000 |
| Requirements | Seattle business, 12+ months operating |
| Type | Loan subsidy (non-dilutive) |

### How It Works

1. Apply for a loan from participating lender
2. If approved, city covers 20% of principal
3. You pay back 80% of original amount
4. Effectively a 20% discount on your loan

### Best For

- Established Seattle businesses (12+ months)
- Companies needing growth capital
- Those who qualify for bank lending

## How to Stack These Opportunities

### The Funding Ladder

| Stage | Target Funding | Sources |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| Idea | $0-$5K | Pitch competitions, library resources |
| Prototype | $5K-$25K | Dempsey, Cascadia, WTIA resources |
| Early Revenue | $25K-$100K | Seattle Capital Access, SBA Microloans |
| Growth | $100K-$250K | Seattle Angel Conference, AWS Impact |
| Scaling | $250K+ | VC funding (see accelerators) |

### Stacking Strategy Example

**Month 1-3:**
- Apply to WTIA Founder Cohort (free, $10K+ resources)
- Enter Dempsey Competition if eligible ($25K potential)

**Month 4-6:**
- Apply for Seattle Capital Access ($30K loan subsidy)
- Complete WTIA program

**Month 7-12:**
- Apply to Seattle Angel Conference ($150K-$200K)
- Apply for Export Voucher if selling internationally

## Non-Dilutive vs. Equity Funding

### When to Use Non-Dilutive

| Situation | Non-Dilutive Best |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Testing concept | Yes |
| Building prototype | Yes |
| First $50K needed | Yes |
| Keeping control | Yes |
| Slow/steady growth | Yes |

### When to Consider Equity

| Situation | Equity May Be Better |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Need $500K+ quickly | Yes |
| Building for acquisition | Yes |
| Need strategic partners | Yes |
| Rapid scaling required | Yes |
